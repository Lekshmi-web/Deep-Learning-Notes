Distance metrics explainer. Drag two points on a grid and compare Euclidean straight-line distance versus Manhattan city-block distance in real time.

Drag either point (A or B) around the grid. See the two most common distance formulas measured live — Euclidean (straight ruler) and Manhattan (city blocks). KNN uses these to find "nearest" neighbors.
Euclidean distance
—
Straight-line distance (like a ruler). Formula: √((x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²)
Manhattan distance
—
City-block distance (like walking streets). Formula: |x₂-x₁| + |y₂-y₁|
Drag either dot to move it. Watch both distances update.
